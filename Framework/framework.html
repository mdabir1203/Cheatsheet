{{Interview Framework Cheat Sheet}}

STAR Framework

  • Situation: Describe the context within which you performed a job or faced a challenge at work.
    Example: I was working as a project manager on a software development project that was falling behind schedule.
  • Task: Explain the task you had to complete highlighting any specific challenges or constraint (e.g., deadlines, costs, other issues).
    Example: My task was to get the project back on track, dealing with a tight deadline and a limited budget.
  • Action: Describe the specific actions that you took to address the task.
    Example: I reorganized the team, implemented a more effective project management methodology, and renegotiated deadlines with stakeholders.
  • Result: Explain the results that your actions achieved.
    Example: As a result, the project was completed on time and within budget, and the client was very satisfied with our work.

CAR Framework

  • Context: Describe the context of the situation.
    Example: As a software engineer, I was part of a team that was tasked with developing a new feature for our product within a short timeframe.
  • Action: Explain the actions you took to address the situation.
    Example: I took the initiative to break down the feature into smaller tasks, and then distributed these tasks among the team members according to their expertise. I also set up daily stand-up meetings to track progress and address any issues promptly.
  • Result: Share the results of your actions.
    Example: As a result, we were able to deliver the feature on time and received positive feedback from our users. This experience also improved our team's ability to work under pressure and manage our time effectively.

SOAR Framework

  • Situation: Describe the situation that you were in.
    Example: I was leading a team of developers in a startup where we were building a mobile application.
  • Obstacle: Explain any obstacles that you had to overcome.
    Example: We encountered a major bug that was causing the application to crash and we had a deadline approaching.
  • Action: Describe the action you took.
    Example: I organized a focused debugging session with the team, where we systematically isolated and resolved the issue.
  • Result: Explain the result of your actions.
    Example: We were able to fix the bug in time for the deadline, and the application received positive reviews on the app store.

PAR Framework

  • Problem: Describe the problem you faced.
    Example: As a data analyst, I noticed that our reports were taking a long time to generate, which was slowing down decision-making processes.
  • Action: Explain the action you took to solve the problem.
    Example: I decided to investigate the issue and found that the queries we were using to pull data were not optimized. I rewrote the queries and also implemented a caching mechanism to store frequently accessed data.
  • Result: Share the result of your actions.
    Example: As a result, report generation time was reduced by 60%, which greatly improved the efficiency of our decision-making processes.

SPAR Framework

  • Situation: Describe the situation.
    Example: I was working as a front-end developer in a team that was responsible for revamping the user interface of our company's main product.
  • Problem: Explain the problem you faced.
    Example: We received feedback from users that the new interface was not intuitive and they were having difficulty navigating through the application.
  • Action: Describe the action you took.
    Example: I proposed conducting a usability study to understand the issues users were facing. I then used the insights from the study to redesign the interface, focusing on improving navigation and usability.
  • Result: Share the result of your actions.
    Example: As a result, user satisfaction increased by 30% and we saw a significant decrease in support requests related to navigation issues.

GROW Framework

  • Goal: Define a clear goal.
    Example: I aimed to improve the performance of our web application.
  • Reality: Describe the current situation.
    Example: The application was slow and users were complaining.
  • Options: Explore the options.
    Example: I considered several solutions, including server upgrade, code optimization, and using a CDN.
  • Way Forward: Describe the chosen solution and its implementation.
    Example: I decided to optimize the code and use a CDN. The application's performance improved significantly and user complaints decreased.
